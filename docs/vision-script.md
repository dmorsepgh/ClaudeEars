Here's a scenario that probably sounds familiar. You're watching TV, listening to a podcast, or sitting in a meeting, and you want to know, how many times did they actually mention that word? Not an estimate. Not a guess. An exact count, with timestamps, and the full transcript of what was said around it.

That's the problem Claude Ears solves.

Claude Ears is a macOS menu bar app that runs quietly in the background and listens to your system audio in real time. It uses Whisper, a powerful speech recognition model from OpenAI, to transcribe everything it hears and flag every time your target word or phrase shows up.

Here's where it gets interesting. You're not limited to one word. You can track multiple keywords at the same time, separated by a comma. Want to know how often a news segment mentions two different politicians? Done. Tracking brand mentions across a competitor's webinar? Easy. Each keyword gets its own running count, updated live right in your menu bar.

When a hit is detected, you get an instant audio ping, the count updates, and every match gets logged to a markdown file with the exact timestamp and the full transcript of what was said around it. So at the end of a session you have a complete record of every mention, not just the number.

The whole thing runs locally. No cloud, no subscription, no data leaving your machine. Whisper runs on your hardware, and the results stay in your notes folder.

It started as a simple command line script and grew into a proper macOS app with launch at login, session logging, and multi-keyword tracking. Built for anyone who needs to pay close attention to what's actually being said.
